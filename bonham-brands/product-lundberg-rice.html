Our Evaluation

Why We Chose Lundberg Family Farms

Rice is one of the most arsenic-contaminated staple foods in the American diet. Almost every sample of rice tested by independent researchers contains detectable arsenic — it is unavoidable because rice absorbs arsenic from soil and water more readily than most other crops. Given that reality, the question isn't whether a rice contains arsenic, but how much, how transparent the brand is about it, and whether the growing location minimizes the risk.

Lundberg Family Farms earns its Bonham Gold Label for three reasons: California sourcing, a decade-long commitment to published third-party heavy metals testing, and a Regenerative Organic Certification that explicitly prohibits glyphosate. Their long grain white rice tested at approximately 65 ppb total arsenic in Mamavation's independent testing — among the lowest results for any long grain white rice tested. Lead was non-detectable.

We are transparent that arsenic is detectable in this product. It is lower than virtually every competitor, and Lundberg is one of the only rice brands that has published its own testing data since 2013. That honesty is exactly what a Bonham Gold Label product looks like.

Per Lundberg Family Farms

  • Regenerative Organic Certified® — the highest regenerative certification, requiring USDA Organic as a baseline and adding soil health, animal welfare, and farmer fairness criteria
  • Explicitly prohibits glyphosate and all toxic chemicals under the Regenerative Organic Certified standard
  • Third-party heavy metals testing conducted at AGQ Labs (accredited) since 2013 — results published publicly on their website
  • 11-year average inorganic arsenic: 0.09 ppm — well below FDA, EFSA, and CODEX maximum levels
  • Cadmium average: less than 13 ppb — well below EU and CODEX maximums
  • No chemical fumigants — food-grade CO2 used to keep stored rice fresh
  • California grown — California soils consistently produce rice with lower arsenic than southern US growing regions
