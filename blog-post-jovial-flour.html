Why We Chose Jovial Einkorn Flour — And the Truth About What's in Most Flour

Flour is one of the most contaminated staple ingredients in the American kitchen. Heavy metals, pesticide residues, bleaching chemicals — the independent testing data is alarming. Here is what we found, why Jovial earned its place, and what we are being fully transparent about.

Why We Chose It

The Bonham Conclusion

We chose Jovial Organic Einkorn All-Purpose Flour because it is the cleanest wheat flour identified by independent third-party laboratory testing — and because the grain itself, einkorn, is fundamentally different from the modern wheat that fills most flour bags sold in the United States.

In June 2024, Lead Safe Mama — one of the most rigorous independent consumer goods testing organizations in the country — tested Jovial Organic Einkorn All-Purpose Flour and reported non-detect results for all four heavy metals they test for: lead, cadmium, mercury, and arsenic. It was the first flour in their entire testing program to achieve that result. In December 2025, a retest showed trace levels of lead and cadmium just above the 5 ppb detection threshold — very low, but detectable.

We are being fully transparent about both results. The 2025 trace detection is why we score Jovial's heavy metals honestly rather than with a perfect score. But when we compare those results to every other flour independently tested — including organic flours, gluten-free flours, and name-brand alternatives — Jovial remains the cleanest option available with published independent verification. That, combined with its Glyphosate Residue Free certification, Regenerative Organic Certification, Non-GMO verification, and the inherent purity of einkorn as an ancient unhybridized grain, is why it earned a Bonham Gold Label.

Who They Are

Jovial Foods — What Makes Them Different

Jovial Foods sources their einkorn exclusively from small organic family farms in Italy, where einkorn has been cultivated for centuries. Einkorn — Triticum monococcum — is the oldest cultivated wheat variety known to exist. It has never been hybridized, never been selectively bred for yield at the expense of nutritional integrity, and never been genetically modified in any form. It is, by definition, the most ancient and unaltered wheat available.

Modern commercial wheat, by contrast, has been extensively crossbred and modified since the mid-twentieth century Green Revolution to produce higher yields and better milling characteristics. This hybridization changed the gluten structure, starch composition, and overall nutritional profile of the grain in ways that many researchers believe contribute to the rise in gluten sensitivity and digestive issues in modern populations.

Jovial's einkorn is stone-milled — a slower, lower-heat process that preserves more of the grain's natural nutrition compared to high-speed roller milling used for conventional flour. The all-purpose flour is produced by stone-milling whole einkorn berries and then sifting to remove some — but not all — of the bran, retaining a portion of the germ and bran that is stripped entirely from conventional white flour.

On testing and transparency: Jovial certifies every harvest as Glyphosate Residue Free through The Detox Project — an independent ISO-accredited laboratory program. Their full certification listing is publicly available. However Jovial does not publish batch-level heavy metals testing results the way Vera Salt does. That distinction is reflected in their Verified score.

Claims & Certifications

What Jovial Claims — And What Each One Means

Certified Glyphosate Residue Free — The Detox Project
The Detox Project is an independent ISO-accredited laboratory program. Glyphosate Residue Free certification means the product was independently lab-tested and confirmed to have no detectable glyphosate or its metabolite AMPA at the lowest government-recognized detection limits. This is not a brand claim — it is a documented lab result. Jovial certifies every harvest. Their listing is publicly verifiable at detoxproject.org. This matters enormously for flour because glyphosate is commonly used as a pre-harvest desiccant on conventional wheat crops — sprayed directly on the grain shortly before harvest to speed drying, leaving residues in the final flour.
Regenerative Organic Certified
Regenerative Organic Certification is the most comprehensive agricultural certification available. It requires USDA Organic as a baseline, then adds soil health requirements, animal welfare standards, and farmer fairness criteria that most organic brands never meet. The certification explicitly prohibits glyphosate and all synthetic toxic pesticides. It is audited by third parties. For flour, this means the soil the einkorn was grown in is being actively improved rather than depleted — healthier soil means less heavy metal bioavailability and cleaner crops over time.
USDA Organic — EU Standards
Jovial's einkorn is grown in Italy under EU organic standards, which are notably stricter than USDA organic requirements in several respects — including lower allowable pesticide residue limits and broader input restrictions. EU organic certification provides a higher baseline of protection than US organic alone.
Non-GMO Verified
Einkorn is inherently non-GMO — it is an ancient grain that has never been genetically modified. Jovial's Non-GMO verification confirms this explicitly. There is no commercially available GMO einkorn anywhere in the world. The certification is belt-and-suspenders confirmation of something that is already true by nature.
Unbleached and Unbromated
Conventional flour is bleached with chlorine or benzoyl peroxide to achieve a white color quickly, and bromated with potassium bromate — a known carcinogen banned in the European Union, Canada, and many other countries but still permitted in the United States — to improve dough handling. Jovial uses neither. The flour's color comes entirely from the grain itself.

The Broader Picture

Why Most Flour — Including Organic — Is More Contaminated Than You Think

Heavy metals in American wheat flour are a systemic problem that the independent testing data makes impossible to ignore. Lead Safe Mama's comparative testing found that 87.5% of organic wheat flours tested positive for heavy metals — cadmium in particular. The source is not primarily the grain itself but the industrial milling equipment used to process it. Cadmium plating is common on mechanical components, and high-speed roller mills used for commercial flour production introduce cadmium contamination at the point of processing. As Lead Safe Mama noted after testing King Arthur Organic Unbleached Bread Flour, Gold Medal All-Purpose Flour, and Costco's Organic Unbleached All-Purpose Flour — all American-produced wheat flours they have tested have shown positive for cadmium at concerning levels. The full comparative data is available at Lead Safe Mama's Flour Chart.

Glyphosate in conventional flour is the result of a widespread industry practice of using glyphosate as a pre-harvest desiccant on wheat crops. Glyphosate is sprayed directly on the standing grain 7 to 14 days before harvest to kill the plant uniformly and speed the drying process. This means the chemical is applied at maximum concentration directly to the food crop — not just to soil or earlier in the growing season. Independent testing by Mamavation and The Detox Project has confirmed detectable glyphosate residues in a significant proportion of conventional and non-certified wheat flours sold in the United States. The Environmental Working Group's analysis of EWG-commissioned testing found glyphosate in oat-based products at levels above their health benchmark for children — a concern that extends to wheat as well. More at EWG — Glyphosate in Food.

Bleaching and bromating agents — chlorine, benzoyl peroxide, and potassium bromate — are routinely added to conventional all-purpose flour to improve its appearance and handling characteristics. Potassium bromate is classified as a possible human carcinogen by the International Agency for Research on Cancer and is banned in the EU, UK, Canada, Brazil, and China. It remains legal in the United States. Most consumers have no idea it may be in their flour. Jovial uses none of these.
